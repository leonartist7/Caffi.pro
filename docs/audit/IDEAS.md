# Ideas Chapter — beyond the plan (read LAST, build never before Phase 5 ships)

Improvements we haven't discussed. Ambitious but honest about effort
(S = days, M = 1–2 weeks, L = a month+). Nothing here belongs in the MVP.

## Product

1. **"The Regular Wall" counter display (M).** A small tablet/screen facing the
   room: first names of today's returning regulars + streaks ("Maya — 12 Tuesdays").
   Diners feel _seen_ (the wow moment made ambient), and it's a live sales demo for
   every customer in line. Opt-in via consent flag; runs off the existing events table.
2. **Cadence-aware nudge timing (S).** You already derive each member's personal
   rhythm for fading detection — use the same math to _send_ at their natural hour
   ("Tuesday 8am person" gets Monday 7pm nudge). Measurably better than blast timing,
   zero new infra.
3. **Usual-aware one-tap reorder on the pass (M).** Wallet pass back-field deep-links
   to "your usual, ready at 8:05" — the parked `/shop` ordering code becomes a
   _single-item_ flow (no cart, no menu browsing). Resurrects the biggest dormant
   asset in the repo at 10% of its scope.
4. **Post-lapse exit whisper (S).** When someone crosses to "lost," one final,
   dignity-preserving message ("no points, no pitch — did we do something wrong?"
   one-tap reply). Owners get the _why_ they never had; the diagnostic's
   `usually-dont-notice` grief, closed.
5. **Gift-a-regular (M).** Members can send a friend one of _their_ earned rewards.
   Referral mechanics disguised as generosity — on-brand acquisition with no discount smell.

## Operations

6. **Onboarding-in-a-box generator (M).** From the brand kit: auto-generated printed
   QR table-tents, counter cards, and the 60-second staff how-to (PDF via server-side
   render). Turns the 14-day concierge into a 2-day one; the checklist already exists in HQ.
7. **Health-board auto-triage (S).** HQ board already tracks falling signups — add
   rule-based playbook suggestions ("signups fell after staff change → offer retraining
   visit") drawn from the events spine. Your churn defense before churn.
8. **Venue "pulse" SMS to the owner (S).** Skip the dashboard entirely some weeks:
   Friday text with THE number + one tap to approve pending drafts via magic link.
   Owner attention budget: 30 seconds, not 5 minutes.

## Revenue

9. **Zone waitlist with deposits (S).** Exclusivity is the moat — make it visible:
   public zone map ("Kensington: full — join waitlist"), refundable deposit to hold
   a spot. Scarcity becomes a lead engine and validates pricing before you knock.
10. **Anonymous neighbourhood benchmark (M).** "Cafés like yours in NW Calgary see
    31% return rate; you're at 24%." Data you'll have anyway; the single most
    renewal-driving number a $149/mo product can show. (Strict k-anonymity, opt-in.)
11. **Roaster/supplier co-op tier (L).** Local roasters sponsor perks ("free bag at
    20 visits") across member cafés — they get counter presence, you get a second
    payer per venue and the distribution channel the strategy doc already flags.

## Elevating the vision

12. **The aro annual: "Your Year at [Café]" (M).** Spotify-Wrapped for regulars each
    December — visits, usual, streaks, "you were our 3rd most loyal Tuesday person."
    Shareable image = organic acquisition; venues get a churn-proofing gift to give.
13. **Portable identity across the aro network (L, phase-3+).** One diner, one pass,
    recognized at every aro café — "the neighbourhood club" literally. Massive
    network-effect play; needs careful per-venue data walls (org-level member linking
    is already in the schema design).
14. **Owner co-op data council (S, non-code).** Invite the first 10 owners into a
    quarterly "what should aro build" circle. Retention, roadmap truth, and the
    testimonial engine the strategy doc says you're missing — for the price of coffee.
